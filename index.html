Overview

Cardiocerebrovascular diseases including heart attacks and strokes are a major cause of death and disability. They are acute events necessitating early diagnosis and intervention for survival and better outcomes. They can happen to anyone, of any age, at any time

32%

Global deaths are due to cardiocerebrovascular diseases

18M

People die every year from cardiocerebrovascular diseases

FACTS

Around 5.5 million people die of cerebrovascular disease worldwide

USA
  • Someone suffers a stroke every 40 second.
  • Someone dies every 4 minutes as a result of stroke.
  • Medical cost for stroke to rise upto $183.13 billion by 2030.
UK
  • >100,000 strokes per year.
  • One stroke every five minutes
  • Annual cost to rise upto £43 billion in 2025.
INDIA
  • 1.5 million strokes every year.
  • Up to 45% patients die within a week after stroke.
  • Increase in economic loss upto $54B in 2015.

A shorter interval between symptom onset and treatment is associated with better cardiac function

Studies show that delaying treatment by 30 minutes reduced average life expectancy by 1 year.

For every 15 minutes longer than 1 hour in receiving angioplasty for acute myocardial infarction, the odds of death increased 1.6 times.

Evidence suggests that quality improvement in stroke care is hindered by inadequate awareness, poor communication, lack of protocols and delayed treatment.

Healthcare systems around the world are continuously trying to make improvements in assessment, diagnosis and treatment of stroke patients.

Time is brain

  • Early action is essential for reduced morbidity and mortality after stroke.
  • Studies have emphasized the critical need for immediate thrombolysis and interventional thrombectomy to increase thenumbers of treatment and improve the survival rates

Despite more than 2 decades of study, little progress has been made in reducing the delay in seeking treatment for acute heart attack and stroke

Treatment to patients with acute myocardial infarction and acute stroke should be accorded the same degree of urgency as the treatment of cardiac arrest
